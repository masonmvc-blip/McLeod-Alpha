# McLeod Alpha Research Note: July 16, 2026 Trading Room

## Scope and Evidence

Source recording: Day Trade SPY, "Trading Room Video Recording - July 16, 2026." The browser-visible Vimeo transcript was reviewed on July 22, 2026. This is external qualitative research only; it does not modify live execution.

## Session Assessment

The room described a prolonged interaction among one-minute moving averages, a pivot, and S1 support. The session was repeatedly called choppy until price held support and later broke upward through the pivot. The transferable lesson is a state transition: do not treat repeated level interaction as a continuation signal until a defined breakout/reclaim occurs.

## Research Findings

1. **Congestion should be a distinct regime.** Repeated interaction with pivot, S1, and the one-minute 50 EMA was described as a "dance" and as chop. McLeod Alpha should evaluate an observational congestion label using repeated crossings, narrow range, and proximity to clustered references.

2. **Breakout acceptance is more meaningful than first contact.** The room waited for a pivot break/new high after earlier uncertainty. Test whether a close and hold beyond a level, rather than a single penetration, improves continuation expectancy.

3. **Fast movement into resistance is not automatically entry quality.** The source explicitly questioned entering after a rapid EMA push. A distance-to-friction and impulse-extension feature may reduce late entries into nearby resistance.

4. **No change to live strategy follows.** These are replay hypotheses; they require option-mark telemetry and out-of-sample evidence before any entry or exit rule changes.

## Candidate Replay Experiments

1. Label `CONGESTION` from pivot/S1/VWAP/EMA proximity, crossing frequency, realized range, and failed breakouts; compare it with baseline entry outcomes.
2. Test a `BREAKOUT_ACCEPTED` feature requiring a close beyond the reference level plus a follow-up hold or new intraday high.
3. Compare target achievement after impulse entries whose initial target path crosses nearby structure against entries with clear room to target.

## Decision

The July 16 evidence supports research on congestion detection and breakout acceptance. It does not justify a live parameter change.
