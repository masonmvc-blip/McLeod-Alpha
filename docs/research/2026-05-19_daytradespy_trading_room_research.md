## Observations

- A rebound from a lower structural area was recognized only after a sharp rejection and improving volume. The room also contrasted that localized bounce with the broader session downtrend, which continued to create lower highs and resistance at moving averages.

- On the downside, the discussion used a measured-move concept, prior lows, pivot support, and an older open gap as nearby structural references. It repeatedly noted the risk of being trapped between support and declining moving averages.
- By the end, the source described a normal-pullback hypothesis on the larger chart while acknowledging weak intraday breadth and persistent downside pressure. The two horizons were not treated as interchangeable.

## Research Implications

1. Label the early narrow-range failures as `NO_ADMISSION` until a break has close-side confirmation, follow-through, and sufficient room to the next opposing structure.
2. Test whether reversal-volume spikes at a defined support zone predict only a local bounce or a durable `STRUCTURAL_RECLAIM` when the larger intraday trend remains negative.
3. Mark scheduled releases as `EVENT_WINDOW` and measure post-release whipsaw, structural hold/failure, and option execution cost separately from ordinary minutes.
4. Record intraday and higher-timeframe states independently; a broader pullback thesis must not overwrite a short-horizon trend or execution constraint in replay.
