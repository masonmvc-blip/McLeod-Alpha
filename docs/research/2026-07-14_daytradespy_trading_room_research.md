# McLeod Alpha Research Note: July 14, 2026 Trading Room

## Scope and Evidence

Source recording: Day Trade SPY, "Trading Room Video Recording - July 14, 2026." The browser-visible Vimeo transcript was reviewed on July 22, 2026. This is external qualitative research only and does not modify live execution.

## Session Assessment

The room identified a high-confluence support area around the pivot and five-minute 50 EMA, produced a quick opening call result, and then faced repeated target approaches/rejections. The useful distinction is between a validated support bounce and discretionary reloading or averaging when a target is nearly reached but not achieved.

## Research Findings

1. **Separate validated bounce from re-entry temptation.** A test and reclaim of support may define an entry, but a prior target miss does not itself validate reloading or averaging. McLeod Alpha should retain its independent entry and risk gates.

2. **Near-target failure is an analyzable state.** A trade that approaches but fails to reach target before returning to support should be labeled with MFE, time-at-high, pullback depth, and structural context. That supports evidence-based exit and re-entry testing later.

3. **Close-based confirmation is recurrent.** The room repeatedly waited for a bar close around a key moving-average barrier. Test close/hold confirmation against intrabar-only break signals.

4. **No live changes follow.** All external source guidance remains research-only until replay and out-of-sample tests validate it.

## Candidate Replay Experiments

1. Label `NEAR_TARGET_REJECTION` using target proximity, MFE, reversal depth, and elapsed time; compare exit outcomes and re-entry quality.
2. Test support-confluence bounces only when a subsequent bar closes and holds above the defining reference.
3. Compare baseline versus a research-only penalty for fresh entries after an unresolved target rejection in the same local structure.

## Decision

The July 14 evidence supports support-confluence, close confirmation, and near-target-rejection research. It does not support automatic averaging, reloading, or any live parameter change.
