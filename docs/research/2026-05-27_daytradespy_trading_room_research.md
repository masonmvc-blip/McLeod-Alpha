# McLeod Alpha Research Report: May 27, 2026 Trading Room

## Scope and Evidence

Source recording: Day Trade SPY, "Trading Room Video Recording - May 27, 2026" (1:13:35). The Vimeo caption transcript was reviewed from the authenticated recording page. This is external qualitative research, not a live trading instruction.

## Observations

- An overnight headline-related spike was substantially retraced before the open. The room recognized that the first five-minute range was indecisive and excluded its opening-range setup rather than force a direction.
- A later downside move was considered only after support/pivot failure and the absence of a durable recovery. The recording repeatedly described nearby prior support as a reason to expect snapbacks and avoid assuming uninterrupted continuation.
- The subsequent upside recovery was assessed through a sequence of support hold, moving-average reclaim, volume improvement, and a break above an intervening structure. The room also treated the initial recovery target as a place to reassess rather than a guarantee of continuation.
- Several later patterns were explicitly ambiguous: a recovery could be a structural reversal or merely a retracement inside the larger session decline. The room monitored volume and the relation to the pivot instead of relying on a single pattern label.
- The session was exposed to macro headlines and a scheduled release. Commentary about geopolitical or macro causes is qualitative event context only; the transcript itself showed that headline effects could reverse quickly.

## Research Implications

1. Label a wide, two-sided opening range as `NO_ADMISSION` unless a later break is accepted by close, structure, and volume.
2. Test `STRUCTURAL_RECLAIM` separately from a short-covering or retracement state by recording the larger intraday swing, pivot side, and post-reclaim follow-through.
3. Measure whether downside continuation after a support break is materially improved by a failed reclaim versus first penetration alone.
4. Add an event-window label for headline-sensitive sessions and compare the stability of technical signals inside and outside that window.

## Decision

No live entry, exit, stop, sizing, or directional policy changes. This recording supports bounded replay of accepted breaks, failed reclaims, and headline-window instability only.
