# McLeod Alpha Research Report: May 13, 2026 Trading Room

## Scope and Evidence

Source recording: Day Trade SPY, "Trading Room Video Recording - May 13, 2026". The Vimeo caption transcript was reviewed from the authenticated recording page. This is external qualitative research, not a live trading instruction.

## Observations

- A sharply inflationary PPI surprise produced an immediate decline. The room first identified downside continuation and then separately watched for evidence of exhaustion, rather than assuming the initial reaction would determine the whole session.
- The opening breakdown was assessed against a pivot and nearby support. The source explicitly noted tension between a model-driven downside break and a developing reversal structure, making confirmation especially important.
- A short downside move was followed by a recovery sequence: support held, price reclaimed moving-average structure, and the later advance accelerated after clearing nearby resistance.
- The room described a potential inverse-head-and-shoulders-style recovery and MACD divergence before treating the upside path as more credible. This was paired with emphasis on the 50-period moving average, volume, and next-week option liquidity.
- The session ended with both an early downside pattern and a later upside continuation reaching their described objectives, illustrating that opening direction alone was not sufficient to characterize the session.

## Research Implications

1. Compare `OPENING_BREAK_CONFIRMED` with `STRUCTURAL_RECLAIM` on high-impact inflation sessions; the same session can contain both a valid early displacement and a later reversal.
2. Capture the order of events: support test, momentum divergence, moving-average reclaim, volume expansion, and resistance clearance.
3. Treat moving-average alignment as a confirmatory state rather than an independent signal; test whether alignment after a support hold improves reversal persistence.
4. Record option liquidity separately by expiry because the source contrasted nearer and later expirations based on spread and volume.

## Decision

No live policy changes. This evidence supports a replay study of whether post-displacement structural reclaims outperform simple continuation assumptions after inflation surprises.
