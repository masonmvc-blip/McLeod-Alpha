# McLeod Alpha Research Report: June 8, 2026 Trading Room

## Scope and Evidence

Source recording: Day Trade SPY, "Trading Room Video Recording - June 8, 2026" (1:12:08). The browser-visible Vimeo transcript was reviewed on July 22, 2026. This is external qualitative research, not a live trading instruction.

## Observations

- The room defined an opening range with support and resistance, then waited for a five-minute close outside it before considering direction; one-minute behavior was used as a confirmation layer.
- A downside opening trade encountered immediate countertrend pressure after entry. The room recognized that the projected pattern was no longer behaving as expected and reduced exposure rather than relying on the original target.
- Later directional attempts were repeatedly rejected inside a narrow range. The source explicitly distinguished a tradable break from oscillation between nearby support and resistance.
- A later upside opportunity was attributed to a higher-volume break above the one-minute 50-EMA, followed by a pullback that held the break; the next resistance was used as the practical objective.
- Option selection commentary considered liquidity, bid-ask spread, and delta alongside the chart premise. Rapid premium changes were visible even while the underlying moved only modestly.

## Research Implications

1. Label an opening breakout as valid only after the five-minute close and a one-minute confirmation condition.
2. Compare pattern-target exits with premise-failure exits when price reclaims the invalidation structure before target.
3. Add range-state and option-liquidity context to replay records so repeated unconfirmed attempts are separable from accepted breaks.

## Decision

No live entry, exit, stop, sizing, or directional policy changes. The useful output is a replayable opening-confirmation and premise-failure hypothesis.
