## Scope and Evidence

Source recording: Day Trade SPY, "Trading Room Video Recording - July 10, 2026." The browser-visible Vimeo transcript was reviewed on July 22, 2026. This is external qualitative research only and does not modify live execution.

## Session Assessment

Later, it explicitly described the tape as range-bound when price failed to close above the one-minute 50 EMA. The useful research distinction is accepted recovery versus unresolved range.

- A bounce from the one-minute 20 EMA and five-minute pre-market support was followed by a requested close above the one-minute 50 EMA. This is a measurable confirmation sequence.
- As price approached the pivot, targets were compressed and gains were taken before the original objective when continuation confidence weakened.
- Later commentary identified wide 15-minute EMAs, rejection at 753.20/R1, a 50-EMA break, and a range-bound one-minute chart. These are candidate extension and congestion conditions.

## Research Findings

1. **Recovery confirmation should precede continuation admission.** Test a sequence of support hold, short-horizon EMA reclaim, and close above the next friction reference.
2. **Target compression should be structural.** When pivot/R1 lies between entry and target, compare a capped target with the original objective using executable option marks.
3. **Use failed close acceptance to identify congestion.** A penetration that cannot close above the one-minute 50 EMA, combined with repeated range crossings, is a candidate `CONGESTION` feature.
4. **No live changes follow.** The recording supplies replay hypotheses only.

## Decision

July 10 supports research on recovery acceptance, structural target compression, and failed-close congestion labels. It does not justify a live parameter change.
