# McLeod Alpha Research Note: July 15, 2026 Trading Room

## Scope and Evidence

Source recording: Day Trade SPY, "Trading Room Video Recording - July 15, 2026." The browser-visible Vimeo transcript was reviewed on July 22, 2026. This is external qualitative research only and does not modify live execution.

## Session Assessment

The room saw an early upside surge, but repeatedly differentiated a completed opening move from later extension risk. Subsequent trades were conditioned on pullbacks to moving averages, consolidation, and a break through nearby resistance. The session also contained a directly relevant data-quality observation: a reported option high may represent one isolated fill and not an executable exit price.

## Research Findings

1. **Executable option telemetry is a prerequisite.** The source's caveat about a one-lot high independently reinforces the existing need to persist bid, ask, mark, high/low since entry, and timestamps. Last trade alone cannot support exit-quality analysis.

2. **EMA separation is a possible extension-risk feature.** The room described an upside move with widely separated moving averages as lacking reliable support. Test normalized price-to-EMA and EMA-spread measures against continuation performance.

3. **Conflicting timeframes should reduce admission confidence.** The source distinguished supportive one-minute structure from a five-minute barrier. McLeod Alpha should test a multi-timeframe alignment score rather than treating all short-horizon momentum equally.

4. **No live changes follow.** These hypotheses require replay validation and executable option-mark data before affecting entry or exit logic.

## Candidate Replay Experiments

1. Compare entries by normalized EMA separation and distance from the nearest short-horizon mean.
2. Test a multi-timeframe agreement feature based on one- and five-minute trend, support/resistance room, and breakout state.
3. Analyze exit feasibility using bid/ask/mark MFE and MAE, explicitly excluding isolated last-trade highs from target-achievement claims.

## Decision

The July 15 evidence supports better option telemetry, extension-risk measurement, and multi-timeframe alignment research. It does not justify a live parameter change.
