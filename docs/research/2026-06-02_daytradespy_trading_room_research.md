# McLeod Alpha Research Report: June 2, 2026 Trading Room

## Scope and Evidence

Source recording: Day Trade SPY, "Trading Room Video Recording - June 2, 2026" (1:16:52). The Vimeo caption transcript was reviewed on July 22, 2026. This is external qualitative research, not a live trading instruction.

## Observations

- A soft open within a broader upward thesis was treated as a possible buyer-rebuild condition, but the room still waited for breaks, retests, and price acceptance around the opening range and pivot.
- Multiple candidate upside entries were delayed while price tested the 50-EMA, nearby Fibonacci levels, and prior highs; patience was explicitly preferred over acting during the pullback.
- The session repeatedly highlighted that option bid-ask spreads constrained otherwise favorable underlying moves, producing modest outcomes even after technical progress.
- The room reduced an objective after a protracted, narrow-range fight and later exited another position at breakeven when the session was nearing its end and the contract had limited remaining time.
- A later breakout extended through structural targets, but follow-through remained uneven and required attention to volume and option execution rather than the chart alone.

## Research Implications

1. Compare opening-pullback entries admitted after retest acceptance with entries taken before the retest completes.
2. Persist option spread, time remaining, and elapsed trade time beside underlying-chart features.
3. Test whether narrow-range duration and late-session timing justify a lower confidence label or a no-admission state.

## Decision

No live entry, exit, stop, sizing, or directional policy changes. The useful output is a replayable retest-quality and option-execution-cost hypothesis.
