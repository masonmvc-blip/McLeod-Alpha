## Session Assessment

The room began with a rapid opening call that met a stated 6% objective, then later managed a 753-call idea through an abrupt headline-driven selloff and recovery attempt. The transferable point is state awareness: a headline shock invalidates the prior smooth-continuation context, and recovery should require a new sequence of structural confirmation rather than an assumption that the original target remains valid.

## Research Findings

1. **Create an event-shock/recovery research label.** A rapid adverse impulse should reset the previous continuation context. Test recovery only after fresh structure confirms pivot reclaim, EMA acceptance, and adequate volume/room.

2. **Opening velocity remains a separate setup feature.** The recurring 6% opening outcome should be analyzed by time-to-target and immediate adverse excursion, not generalized to later trades.

3. **Separate underlying signal context from option execution telemetry.** Underlying price structure can determine setup eligibility, while bid/ask/mark and option excursions determine executable entry/exit assessment.

4. **No live changes follow.** This external observation remains a replay hypothesis until validated against McLeod Alpha's own data.
