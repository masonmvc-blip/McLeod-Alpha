# McLeod Alpha Research Report: June 11, 2026 Trading Room

## Scope and Evidence

Source recording: Day Trade SPY, "Trading Room Video Recording - June 11, 2026" (1:14:49). The browser-visible Vimeo transcript was reviewed on July 22, 2026. This is external qualitative research, not a live trading instruction.

## Observations

- Mixed producer-price and employment data were recognized as uncertainty context, while the room still used opening support, resistance, moving averages, and volume to determine the immediate setup.
- The opening downside break generated an initial put position, but the market quickly reclaimed the structure. The room later acknowledged that a break-even or reduced-loss exit was preferable once the original downside thesis no longer had clean structural room.
- A subsequent upside scalp was taken after a pivot and moving-average recovery. The source exited quickly when price repeatedly rejected nearby resistance rather than waiting for a larger projection.
- The session repeatedly returned to pivot, EMA, Fibonacci, and pitchfork confluence. The source treated confluence as a decision area, not proof that price would reverse at a precise level.
- A later potential continuation trade was deferred because the range was noisy and a clear next setup had not formed.

## Research Implications

1. Test reclaim-after-break as an invalidation label for opening-break candidates.
2. Measure whether confluence improves outcomes only when accompanied by confirmed displacement and enough room to the next friction area.
3. Treat absence of a clear setup as a valid no-trade research state rather than forcing a directional classification.

## Decision

No live entry, exit, stop, sizing, or directional policy changes. The useful output is a replayable invalidation, confluence, and no-trade-state hypothesis.
