## Research Priorities

1. Replay opening moves using structural room, close-side confirmation, post-break hold or reclaim, volume displacement, and the distance to the next reaction zone.
2. Build research-only labels for `OPENING_BREAK_CONFIRMED`, `OPENING_BREAK_RECLAIMED`, `OPENING_FOLLOW_THROUGH`, `POST_BREAK_TEST_HELD`, `STRUCTURAL_RECLAIM`, `PREMISE_FAILURE`, `EXTENDED_IMPULSE`, `FAST_DISPLACEMENT`, `GAP_DISPLACEMENT`, `EVENT_WINDOW`, and `NO_ADMISSION`.
3. Compare continuation against reversal outcomes after immediate support contact on event-driven gaps and inflation releases.
4. Evaluate structural-reclaim and premise-failure outcomes with executable option data, including spread, implied volatility, time decay, MFE, MAE, and time-to-resolution.
5. Require replay improvement, out-of-sample confirmation, and the existing risk-certification process before any live deployment.

## Conclusion

The full five-session block supports a bounded research thesis: opening direction becomes more informative when it survives a close, retest, structural zone interaction, and volume assessment, while rapid event displacement and large overnight gaps make premise failure and structural reclaim especially important. This is a replay and instrumentation agenda, not a live-trading overlay.
