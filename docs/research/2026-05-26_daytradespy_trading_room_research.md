# McLeod Alpha Research Report: May 26, 2026 Trading Room

## Scope and Evidence

Source recording: Day Trade SPY, "Trading Room Video Recording - May 26, 2026" (1:16:03). The Vimeo caption transcript was reviewed from the authenticated recording page. This is external qualitative research, not a live trading instruction.

## Observations

- The post-holiday session opened with a large gap, an early sharp reversal, and uncertainty around a scheduled consumer-confidence release. The room treated the initial move as unstable rather than assuming the gap direction would persist.
- Support, resistance, pivot, and moving-average references were repeatedly handled as areas requiring a reaction, not exact executable prices. A downside idea was explicitly deferred after the sell-off reached support and reversed.
- The favorable source-side setup was described as a support hold followed by an inverted-head-and-shoulders-style recovery, moving-average reclaim, and improving one-minute volume. It was framed as a short-duration opportunity with nearby resistance still relevant.
- Later continuation discussion still emphasized waiting for a pullback, a hold, or a renewed close before acting. A rapid move through resistance was not treated as sufficient reason to chase.
- The source noted a holiday-related chart/pivot anomaly and used manual cross-checking instead of treating the displayed indicator as authoritative. It also mentioned option volume, open interest, and changing spreads during fast movement.

## Research Implications

1. Add a holiday-session context flag and test whether opening support/reclaim behavior differs from ordinary sessions.
2. Treat support/reclaim plus volume and moving-average alignment as a replay candidate, while separately measuring false reclaims and the distance to the next resistance zone.
3. Record data-quality anomalies for chart-derived levels; an unavailable or inconsistent indicator must prevent a level from becoming a trusted feature without independent reconstruction.
4. Capture option bid, ask, spread, volume, and open interest around rapid underlying moves before evaluating any technical setup.

## Decision

No live entry, exit, stop, sizing, or directional policy changes. The useful output is a research-only hypothesis about holiday-session support reclaims, confirmation, and data-quality gating.
