# McLeod Alpha Research Report: June 1, 2026 Trading Room

## Scope and Evidence

Source recording: Day Trade SPY, "Trading Room Video Recording - June 1, 2026" (1:12:38). The Vimeo caption transcript was reviewed on July 22, 2026. This is external qualitative research, not a live trading instruction.

## Observations

- A sharp pre-market selloff was framed as a potential mean-reversion opportunity, but the room repeatedly checked one-minute EMA behavior, volume, and nearby structural resistance before extending the thesis.
- The session showed that a fast recovery can stall repeatedly at Fibonacci, pitchfork, and EMA confluence; the market remained range-bound despite an initially bullish interpretation.
- The room used short-duration option positions around structural objectives, then reduced targets or exited when the expected follow-through did not arrive.
- A scheduled economic release added a discontinuous risk point. The response to the release created rapid reversals that weakened the usefulness of the prior intraday narrative.
- Connectivity problems prompted the source to reduce or exit exposure, illustrating that execution-state integrity is an independent constraint on any chart premise.

## Research Implications

1. Separate a fast pre-market displacement from a validated reversal; require subsequent structural acceptance and volume-supported continuation.
2. Record confluence zones and count failed attempts through them instead of treating the first recovery as a durable trend change.
3. Include scheduled-event proximity and data/execution-health state in replay records.

## Decision

No live entry, exit, stop, sizing, or directional policy changes. The useful output is a replayable reversal-confirmation, event-risk, and execution-health hypothesis.
