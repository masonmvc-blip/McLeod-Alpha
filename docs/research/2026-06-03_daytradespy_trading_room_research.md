## Observations

- The room declined a late downside entry after an extended selloff had already reached measured-move and support areas, even though the immediate move remained negative.
- Scheduled PMI releases and a concurrent Treasury-related event were treated as sources of heightened uncertainty, with the market moving sharply before and around the releases.
- A later recovery attempt was evaluated through Fibonacci retracements, one-minute and five-minute EMA alignment, and volume; a repair/averaging approach was discussed by the source after adverse movement.
- The source also noted that the broader up-leg appeared mature, reinforcing the distinction between a macro directional narrative and an executable intraday setup.

## Research Implications

1. Label failed opening acceptance separately from ordinary pullbacks, using the loss of the breakout level and expansion of adverse displacement.
2. Test an extended-impulse exclusion: do not admit a new directional trade after a measured move reaches stacked support or resistance without a fresh setup.
3. Treat averaging or repair commentary as source behavior to evaluate, not as a candidate live method.

## Decision

No live entry, exit, stop, sizing, averaging, or directional policy changes. The useful output is a replayable failed-break and extended-impulse exclusion hypothesis.
