## Research Priorities

1. Build replay labels for accepted break, failed break, post-break hold, structural reclaim, gap failure, and gap recovery.
2. Separate scheduled-event windows from overnight headline displacement and measure stabilization time, reversal depth, and range expansion.
3. Evaluate whether moving-average/Fibonacci/pivot confluence improves outcomes after controlling for volume and structural room.
4. Add an execution-quality layer that can reject theoretical options results when quote spread, liquidity, or fill-state evidence is inadequate.
5. Compare impulse continuation with pullback/reclaim behavior, particularly when extension and low-volume breakout evidence conflict.

## Conclusion

The week supports a bounded research thesis: price structure becomes more informative when a move survives close-side acceptance, retest, volume evaluation, and executable options conditions. Gaps, event windows, and extended impulses increase the value of measuring failure and reclaim rather than treating the first visible move as a durable signal.
