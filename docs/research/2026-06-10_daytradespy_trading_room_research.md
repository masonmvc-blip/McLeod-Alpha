## Scope and Evidence

Source recording: Day Trade SPY, "Trading Room Video Recording - June 10, 2026" (1:14:07). The browser-visible Vimeo transcript was reviewed on July 22, 2026. This is external qualitative research, not a live trading instruction.

## Observations

- CPI was treated as a catalyst context, while the actual opening decision still depended on price holding the one-minute 50-EMA and the five-minute structure.
- The opening upside setup used a five-minute close beyond the upper range, then a pullback/hold near the rising 10-EMA before entry. Low option volume was explicitly noted as a constraint even when the chart direction looked constructive.
- The room adjusted a nominal target downward when price repeatedly stalled at a known resistance and pitchfork area, treating observed resistance as more important than the original objective.
- Later five-minute AB=CD discussion described the 38% retracement as the minimum pullback for the pattern and stressed that five-minute patterns contain more intraday noise than one-minute patterns.
- Risk commentary rejected allowing a single position to erase prior gains. A continuing-position test asked whether the trade would still be initiated at the current price and context.

## Research Implications

1. Separate event context from directional admission: event information can condition uncertainty without replacing price confirmation.
2. Test target adjustment at nearby structural resistance against static-target handling, using executable option prices.
3. Record option liquidity and spread state at admission; directional correctness alone may not make a setup executable.

## Decision
