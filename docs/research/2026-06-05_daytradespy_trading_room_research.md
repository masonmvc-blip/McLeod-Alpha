# McLeod Alpha Research Report: June 5, 2026 Trading Room

## Scope and Evidence

Source recording: Day Trade SPY, "Trading Room Video Recording - June 5, 2026" (1:09:55). The Vimeo caption transcript was reviewed on July 22, 2026. This is external qualitative research, not a live trading instruction.

## Observations

- Stronger-than-expected employment data coincided with a substantial early decline. The room initially pursued downside option scalps but recognized progressively stronger support as price approached layered Fibonacci, pivot, and pitchfork zones.
- The source took short-duration gains on downside positions as the move extended, then waited for a potential double bottom and moving-average recovery before considering calls.
- A later upside scalp was admitted only after price held the short-term averages and pushed through the one-minute 50-EMA; the target was set near the five-minute 20-EMA rather than assumed open-ended upside.
- A subsequent rally stalled below the next moving-average/structural level, showing that a technically valid bounce can still be bounded by overhead friction.
- The room retained older call exposure over the weekend based on a discretionary macro thesis. That holding decision is separate from the intraday evidence and is not adopted.

## Research Implications

1. Label impulse exhaustion when a directional move reaches a cluster of independent structural supports or resistances.
2. Test reversal admission only after a double-bottom or equivalent base, short-term EMA acceptance, and a volume-supported break.
3. Keep multi-session discretionary holds distinct from intraday setup evaluation and require separate evidence for either category.

## Decision

No live entry, exit, stop, sizing, holding-period, or directional policy changes. The useful output is a replayable exhaustion-to-reversal hypothesis.
