# McLeod Alpha Research Report: 2026-03-31 Trading Room

## Scope and Evidence

Source recording: Day Trade SPY, "Trading Room Video Recording - March 31, 2026." The authenticated Vimeo transcript was reviewed through the session close; this report retains paraphrased synthesis only, not source transcript text. This is external qualitative research, not a live trading instruction.

## Observations

- Late in the session, the room treated a break through a referenced 50-level area as a structural event, then focused on whether price could hold that change rather than assuming a durable move.
- A nearby 38.2% retracement area was named as the next downside test. The discussion used it as a conditional checkpoint, not as proof that the downside path would complete.
- The downside scenario required a retest of the 50-level reference that failed; only then did the room describe monitoring a lower zone. This makes the failed retest the stated invalidation/confirmation bridge between the two areas.
- The presenter described watching the lower test for a possible trade, but the transcript does not provide an executable contract, fill, stop, position size, or outcome.
- Visual chart review, synchronized underlying bars, executable option bid/ask/mark data, and trade-ledger reconciliation remain unavailable, so exact level placement, execution quality, and realized performance are not asserted.

## Research Implications

1. Test `BREAK_RETEST_FAILURE` by marking breaks through a structural reference and measuring whether a failed return test improves identification of the next lower target zone.
2. Test `RETRACEMENT_CHECKPOINT_382` as a conditional target label: compare first tests of the retracement area after a failed retest with tests reached without that prior failure.
3. Record the break, retest, invalidation, lower-zone arrival, and 1-, 3-, 5-, 10-, and 15-minute forward windows so the claimed sequence can be falsified against underlying data.
4. Require option bid, ask, mark, contract, size, MFE, MAE, and timestamped ledger linkage before any source-described possible trade is evaluated as an execution or risk result.

## Decision

No live entry, exit, stop, sizing, direction, or other trading-policy change is authorized. This session remains research-only; the structural sequence is a replay candidate, not a validated execution rule.
