## Scope and Evidence

## Observations

- The opening was framed as a consolidation following a strong multi-session advance, with both an upper structural reference and a lower support boundary identified before the session developed.
- An initial downside break was treated cautiously and later showed loss of downside momentum near support. The room repeatedly updated its interpretation as price moved between the one-minute and five-minute moving-average clusters.
- A lower-range break eventually received a close-based confirmation, but the subsequent decline slowed near prior close/support and volume behavior prompted partial risk reduction in the narration.
- A later reclaim through the one-minute moving-average complex produced a fast upward response. The presenters acknowledged that the same session could support competing short-term narratives, depending on whether the next structural test held.
- The transcript stresses uncertainty under headline risk and treats several chart formations as possibilities rather than determinate predictions.

## Research Implications

- Use `BREAK_CONFIRMATION` only when a break survives a close and the next microstructure test; measure the distinction between an initial break, a failed break, and a sustained extension.
- Add `PRIOR_CLOSE_PROXIMITY` and nearby support density as features for post-break deceleration. Analyze whether these features correlate with partial reversal or merely temporary pause.
- Label `TIMEFRAME_CONFLICT` whenever one-minute momentum conflicts with five-minute structure. This should be a research-state feature, not an automatic trade directive.
- Preserve headline-risk regime state so apparent technical failures can be stratified by elevated unscheduled-news conditions.
